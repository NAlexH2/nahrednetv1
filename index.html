My name is Alex Harris and I am a senior at Portland State University with an expected graduation date of June 2023.

I am currently pursuing a Bachelors of Science degree in Computer Science with the Maseeh College of Engineering and Computer Science, while also pursuing a minor in Mathematics.

When I've spoken to my friends about how we see one another, a few words that are frequently reccuring descriptors have been both "resilient" and "soft-spoken". Attributes that I never really considered for myself, but am certainly not opposed to. These haven't always been the case, but my experience has certainly helped shape me.

Past:

I found that College after High School was not the path for me - not yet at least. I spent 8 years active duty in the US Navy as an Electronics Technician where I worked on both internal and external means of communication: radios, SONAR, cryptographic equipment and a patch system that managed all operational calls on these systems. By the time my first 4 years had finished I made the enlisted rank of Petty Officer Second Class in a highly competitive job for the Navy.

By the end of my second 4 years, I had worked on and executed multi-million-dollar projects, became the ships expert on technical documentation, and was a highly trusted junior enlisted Sailor overseas in Japan. While there, I was a part of a team responsible for docking and undocking ships, submarines, and barges as well as managing helicopter flight operations.


Present:

My current focus is highly on school. I returned to Portland State as the COVID pandemic started in the Spring of 2020. Between here and there I've taken classes that have taught me foundational programming knowledge but also algorithms and testing methodologies. For more on these please visit the skills page.

During the Summer of 2022, I took 16 credits in order to finalize my graduation date and set my future up to be in a better position than what it would have been. Doing this was hard work and required a lot of dedication to accomplish outstanding grades in a matter of eight weeks for four classes - Calculus 3, Statistics, Introduction to Web Development, and Advanced Programming with Java.
